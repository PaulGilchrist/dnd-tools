# 🐉 D&D Tools — Your Quick-Reference Companion for the Table

**Keep the game moving. Keep the excitement flowing.**

D&D Tools is a fast, browser-based reference application built for Dungeon Masters and players who want instant access to any rule, spell, monster, item, or piece of lore — right in the middle of a session. No flipping through rulebooks. No scrolling through PDFs. Just search, filter, and find what you need in seconds.

Whether you play with the classic **5th Edition (5e)** rules or the updated **2024 Essentials** rules, this app has you covered. Switch between rule versions with a single click from the navigation bar, and every section — spells, monsters, magic items, classes, races, feats — automatically updates to match.

> **The goal is simple:** get out of the way. When you're in the middle of a session, you don't want to fight with your reference tool. You want to find what you need instantly and get back to the story. The app handles the lookup. You handle the adventure.

---

## 🌟 What Makes This Different

This isn't just another rulebook viewer. It's designed specifically for **speed during gameplay**:

- **Instant search** — Find any spell, monster, item, or rule by name
- **Deep filtering** — Narrow down results by CR, rarity, class, level, environment, XP, and more
- **Bookmark your favorites** — Mark spells, monsters, items, and equipment you use often for one-click access
- **Persistent memory** — Your filters, bookmarks, and spell lists survive browser refreshes — no account needed
- **Shareable links** — Send a direct link to any specific entry so others can jump straight to it
- **Two rule versions** — Full parallel support for both 5e and 2024 Essentials, switchable in one click

## 🎯 Features

### 📜 Spells

Browse the complete spell list for both rule versions with powerful filtering and tracking:

- **Filter by** spell level (0–9), casting time, class, and name
- **Mark spells as Known or Prepared** — Track which spells your character has access to and which are prepared for the day. Prepared spells are automatically marked as known.
- **Status filtering** — Show only known, prepared, or unprepared spells
- **Expandable spell cards** — See full details including range, components, duration, and higher-level effects
- **Separate tracking per rule version** — Your 5e and 2024 spell lists are tracked independently
- **Direct links** — Share a link to any specific spell, and it opens expanded for the recipient

### 👾 Monsters

Three distinct ways to explore the monster compendium:

**Search** — Find any monster with powerful multi-axis filters:
- Filter by **Challenge Rating** range, **XP** range, **size**, **type**, and **environment**
- Search by **name** for instant lookups
- **Bookmark** your favorite monsters for quick access — filter to show only bookmarked monsters
- Full stat blocks with ability scores, armor class, hit points, actions, bonus actions, reactions, legendary actions, lair actions, regional effects, and special abilities
- Source book and page references for every monster

**Lore** — Browse monsters organized by type and subtype:
- Expand a monster type (Dragon, Undead, Fiend, etc.) to see its lore description and all associated monsters
- Click any monster within a type to see its full stat block
- *2024 version* includes both monster types and subtypes for even more granular organization
- Perfect for world-building and understanding monster ecosystems

**Encounters** — Build and balance encounters on the fly:
- Set your **party composition** — Add multiple players of different levels
- Choose a **difficulty** target (Easy, Medium, Hard, Deadly)
- Select monsters from a filtered list and adjust quantities with +/− controls
- See **real-time XP calculations** — Total XP, monster count, difficulty multiplier, effective XP, and the resulting encounter difficulty rating
- Search and filter monsters within the encounter builder by name, type, or subtype
- Clear all selections and start over with one click
- Filters persist between sessions so your last encounter setup is always there

### 🎒 Magic Items

Complete magic item reference for both rule versions:

- **Filter by** rarity (Common, Uncommon, Rare, Very Rare, Legendary), type, attunement requirements, and name
- **Bookmark** items you use frequently — filter to show only bookmarked items
- Full descriptions including attunement requirements and special properties
- Expandable cards with all details visible at a glance

### ⚔️ Equipment & Weapons

Comprehensive equipment database that adapts to your chosen rule version:

- **Filter by** category (weapons, armor, shields, tools, etc.), name, weapon properties, and range (melee/ranged)
- **Bookmark** frequently used items for quick reference
- **Weapon property descriptions** — Hover to see what Finesse, Heavy, Thrown, Loading, and other properties mean
- Automatically adapts content based on the selected rule version (5e or 2024)

### 📚 Rules Reference

Everything you need from the rulebooks, organized and searchable:

**General Rules** — Full-text search across all rules:
- Search across rule names and descriptions simultaneously
- Navigate between matches with **arrow keys** (↑↓) or **Alt+J/K** for vim-style navigation
- See highlighted matches with context — matching text is highlighted in the results
- Press **Escape** to clear the search
- Source book and page references included with every rule entry

**Ability Scores** — Complete rules for all six abilities, including modifiers, ability checks, and saving throws

**Conditions** — All status effects (blinded, charmed, frightened, paralyzed, petrified, poisoned, prone, restrained, unconscious, invisible, exhaustion, and more) with full mechanical descriptions and how to end them. Content adapts to the selected rule version.

**Feats** — All available feats with prerequisites, benefits, and full descriptions
- *2024 version*: Additional filters by type, repeatable status, minimum level, and ability score requirement

**Races & Subraces** — All playable races with their traits, ability score bonuses, and features
- *5e version*: Races with expandable subrace details
- *2024 version*: Updated races with the new lineage system, including subraces with their own detailed stat blocks

**Player Classes** — Detailed class information for all 12 classes:
- Level-by-level progression tables with all features at each level
- Class features and abilities with prerequisites
- Spellcasting information including spell progression and save DC
- Subclasses (*5e*) / Major options (*2024*) with full feature lists
- Proficiency bonuses, hit dice, and ability score requirements
- *2024 version*: Core traits, level progression, and major options replacing the traditional subclass system

**Backgrounds** (*2024 only*) — The new background system with ability score increases, a starting feat, skill and tool proficiencies, and starting equipment options

**Weapon Mastery** (*2024 only*) — Complete reference for the new weapon mastery system, showing the damage dice and special effects for each weapon type

### 🗺️ Locations

D&D locations with descriptions and source references:

- Expandable cards with full lore descriptions
- **Full-screen map images** — Click the Image button to view large map images for each location
- Source book and page references for deeper research

### 👤 Names Generator

Generate character and building names for your campaign:

- **Character names** by race (Dragonborn, Dwarf, Elf, Gnome, Halfling, Half-Orc, Tiefling, Human Celtic) and sex (male/female)
- **Family names** where available for certain races — Generate both first and last names
- **Building names** for world-building — Generate names for taverns, shops, guildhalls, and more
- **Track used names** — Mark names as used to avoid repetition
- **Filter by usage** — Show only available (unused) names, only used names, or all names

## 💾 Everything Remembers You

All your preferences are saved locally in your browser — no account, no server, no sign-up:

- **Filters persist** — Your last-used filters in every section are restored when you return
- **Bookmarks persist** — Your favorite monsters, items, and equipment stay bookmarked across sessions
- **Spell tracking persists** — Known and prepared spells are remembered between sessions (separately for 5e and 2024)
- **Used names persist** — Your name history carries forward across sessions
- **Encounter settings persist** — Your party composition and difficulty settings are saved

## 🔗 Shareable Links

Every entry in the app has a unique URL. Share a link to a specific spell, monster, item, or rule, and anyone who opens it will see that exact entry expanded and scrolled into view. Perfect for:

- Sending a player a link to a spell they asked about between sessions
- Sharing a monster stat block with your DM
- Linking to a specific rule during a rules debate
- Bookmarking a specific entry in your browser

## 🎲 How to Use It During a Game

### During Play
1. Open the app in your browser (bookmark it for instant access)
2. Switch to the correct rule version (5e or 2024) using the nav bar dropdown
3. Navigate to the section you need — spells, monsters, items, rules
4. Use search and filters to find exactly what you need
5. Expand any card to see full details

### Between Sessions
- **Bookmark** monsters and items you expect to encounter
- **Mark your spells** as known and prepared for upcoming sessions
- **Build test encounters** to balance upcoming sessions using the Encounter Builder
- **Browse monster lore** for inspiration and world-building
- **Generate names** for NPCs and locations, tracking which ones you've used

## 🧩 Companion App: D&D Character Sheet

This app works alongside **[D&D Character Sheet](https://github.com/PaulGilchrist/dnd-char-sheet)** — a companion application for creating, managing, and sharing character sheets with your entire table.

**[Learn more about D&D Character Sheet →](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/PaulGilchrist/dnd-char-sheet/refs/heads/main/README.md)** · **[Download it here →](https://github.com/PaulGilchrist/dnd-char-sheet)**

While **D&D Tools** is your quick-reference guide for rules, spells, monsters, and items, **D&D Character Sheet** handles everything character-related:

- **Character creation wizard** — Step-by-step guided character creation with rule validation
- **Digital character sheets** — Living documents that update as you play, with all stats calculated automatically
- **Real-time sharing** — The DM shares a link, and everyone at the table sees the same character sheets and combat tracker instantly, on their own devices
- **Combat tracking** — Initiative order, turn tracking, round counter, HP management, and battle notes
- **Print-ready sheets** — Beautiful single-page character sheets optimized for the table, with all critical combat stats front and center
- **Campaign management** — Run multiple campaigns, level up characters, download/upload characters between sessions
- **Spell slot tracking** — Click to use spell slots during play, reset after long rests
- **Class resource tracking** — Rage, Ki, Action Surge, Divine Smite, and more — all tracked during play

**Use them together for the complete experience:**
- Use **D&D Tools** to look up rules, spells, monsters, and items during play
- Use **D&D Character Sheet** to manage your characters, track combat, and share everything with your table

Both apps support the same 5e and 2024 rule versions, so they stay in sync with whatever your table is playing.

> **No computer expertise required.** If you can open a web browser, you can use these apps.
